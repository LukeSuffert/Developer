Quiet Resources

Sometimes, we just need a gentle reminder or a small shift to feel more balanced. This page offers habits and tools that support mental well-being without overwhelming your schedule. These suggestions are simple, flexible, and meant to fit into real life, not perfection. Choose what feels right, and know that each small step counts.

Healthy Habits

  1. Write in a Journal (it doesn't have to be perfect — just honestly express your feelings for a couple of minutes every day)
  2. Listen to Calm Sounds or Music
  3. Go for a Walk (even a short one can help clear your mind)
  4. Unplug briefly every day (find 5 minutes to step away from screens)
  5. Create a Bedtime Wind-down Routine (read a book, drink herbal tea, or do some light stretching before bed)

How Can I Add Something to My Routine?

  1. Commit to a Habit
  2. Connect it to Something You Already Do
  3. Keep it Tiny (start with super easy first steps)
  4. Set a Reminder or Visual Cue (sticky notes, phone alarms, calendars, etc.)
  5. Be Kind to Yourself (if you miss a day, just start again tomorrow)

Let’s say you’d like to start journaling but don’t know where to fit it in. You decide to place your journal on your pillow each morning. At night, right before you go to bed, you see it — a soft reminder. Instead of writing a full page, you begin by noting just one sentence about your day. After a few days, this becomes a moment you look forward to. It grows naturally, with no pressure. That’s how habits start to take root.
